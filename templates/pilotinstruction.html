How To Become A Pilot

Step 1: Basic Requirements

To begin your pilot training, you need to meet several basic requirements:

  • Educational Requirements: Minimum secondary education (10+2), with Physics and Mathematics.
  • Medical Fitness: Obtain a Class 1 Medical Certificate, verifying that you meet health standards for flying.
  • Age Requirements: You must be at least 17 years old to start training for a Student Pilot License and 18 years old for a Commercial Pilot License.
  • Legal Requirements: Pass a background check to ensure you have no serious criminal offenses that could disqualify you from flying.
  • Language Proficiency: Proficiency in English is crucial for communication in all aspects of aviation.

Step 2: Ground School Training

Acquire theoretical knowledge necessary for flying. Enroll in a flying school that offers ground school to prepare for DGCA exams.

Step 3: DGCA Exams

Pass the DGCA written exams. Check our DGCA resources page for study materials and tips.

Step 4: Flight Training

Accumulate the required flight hours under the supervision of a certified flight instructor.

Step 5: Advanced Ratings

Obtain additional ratings such as Instrument Rating or Multi-Engine Rating to enhance your skills and employability.

Step 6: Continuous Education

Keep up with ongoing learning and stay updated with new technologies and regulations in aviation.

Step 7: Networking

Networking is key in aviation. Connect with professionals through our pilot contacts page for mentorship and career opportunities.
